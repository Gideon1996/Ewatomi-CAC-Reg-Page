EVERYTHING YOU NEED TO KNOW ABOUT CAC ANNUAL RETURN

Over time, most businesses and company did not know what annual return is, some of them are aware of it while some are of the pinion that it is not important since the business or company has been registered with Corporate Affairs Commission.

In this article, you’ll know everything you need to know about CAC annual return;

  • What is annual return?
  • Who is to file annual return?
  • Disadvantages of not filing annual return
  • Advantages of filing annual return
  • Conclusion

What is CAC annual return?

Like the name implies, “annual return”, it is a document or a payment that is been filed at once a year.

CAC annual return is a report that businesses or companies must send to government each year by giving particular details, for example, the name of business owner, name of company directors, address and details of assets and shares.

Annual return is what will make CAC understands that the business still exists. CAC annual return is been filled every year.

Who is to file annual return?

This is another important question that you’ll want to know as many entities don’t know who is to file annual return. CAC annual return is not for everyone, it is not for all businesses or companies. CAC annual return is only for those who have a registered entity with Corporate Affairs Commission. If you have a business, company, incorporated trustee, or religious organization registered with CAC, it is mandatory for you to file your annual return. So, if you fall in any of the aforementioned, it is compulsory for you to file annual return. Annual return is not optional, it is compulsory for all registered entity.

Disadvantages of not filing annual return

You might be wondering, what might actually be the disadvantages for not filing your CAC annual return. There are some disadvantages attach to not filing it which are;

  1. According to the Company and Allied Matters Act as amended 2020 (CAMA 2020), failure to file annual returns for a consecutive period of 10 years is a ground for striking off the name of such entity from CAC register. The commission will believe that such business or company does not exist anymore. Hence, the commission will strike off the name which means the certificate is no more useful.
  2. The commission will notify the general public by publishing those names in at least 3 national newspapers that the said entity has been strike off and nobody should engage in whatsoever businesses or transactions with them again.
  3. There is late penalty for not filling it at the right time. It must not be delayed. It must be done at least once in a year.
  4. The name won’t be available for registration after it has been strike off. i.e. the proprietor won’t be able to register such name again unless the annual return is been filled up to date.
  5. It will be a disgrace to the entity for not filing annual return and allowing CAC to strike it off.

Advantages of filing annual return

Will you even get anything in return if you file your annual return? Below are some of the advantages.

  1. It makes you a law abiding citizen
  2. It helps the commission to understand that such entity still exists.
  3. It allows you to have access to some government benefits such as survival funds, loan contract and many more.
  4. It saves time whenever such entity wants to do any post incorporation. Post incorporation is any registration that is done after an entity has been registered. For example, upgrading from a business name to Limited Liability Company or Non Governmental Organizations.
  5. When annual return is been paid at the right time, you won’t pay for penalty fee, which means you’ll pay less to those who have default.

In conclusion, filing of annual return is good for your registered entity; it is the one that allows CAC to determine your consistency in business. Once you file it, you’re safe. If you default for 10 years, your entity would be strike off. CAC annual return is to be filed once in a year.

BENEFITS OF REGISTERING YOUR BUSINESS WITH CORPORATE AFFAIRS COMMISSION

Anyone can decide one day to start operating a business. All that the person needs to do is provide a product or service to another person in exchange for money or money’s worth to be “in business.” While registering your business with the” Corporate Affairs Commission” (CAC) isn’t necessarily required to complete certain business transactions, it is a smart choice for new and existing entrepreneurs for a variety of reasons which includes:

  1. Certificate of incorporation by corporate affairs commission (CAC).

    Upon registration of a business, the commission issues a certificate of incorporation to the business owner evidencing that such business has been registered. Registering your business gives you the right to operate that business. Also, the certificate of incorporation provides a small business in Nigeria with a good reputation as well as legal recognition.

  2. Opening Business Bank Accounts

    You need to provide proof that your business is properly registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) to open a business bank account in Nigeria. A business bank account is an important asset to a small business because you can separate your personal activities from your business activities.

  3. Safeguarding Your Business / Corporate Name

    One huge advantage of registration with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) is safeguarding your business name from being used by another person / business. No one else can register a business with that same name or a similar name.

  4. Legal Liability Protection

    Another benefit of registration is receiving some legal liability protection (note that a private company limited by share has a legal personality distinct from that of its subscribers). If you register or incorporate your business, you will not be held personally responsible for certain accidents and other liabilities incurred by the registered company.

  5. Getting Loans

    When you apply for business loans, you’re going to have to prove that you’re actually a business. Banks and investors will ask to see your business registration certificate along with other application requirements before approving your loan application.

  6. Reputation with Customers

    Customers and clients, especially people you’ve never worked with before, need assurance that you are a legitimate business. A potential client may suspect your business of being a “fly-by-night” operation if your company isn’t properly registered. When a business is registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC), it could put your clients at ease when making a decision about whether to spend money with / transact business with your company.

  7. Supplier Arrangements / Government Contracts

    A registered business also makes you eligible to receive supplier discounts that you wouldn’t normally receive as an unregistered business entity. Suppliers usually reserve special rates for business owners who can show official evidence of business name or company registration (certificate of incorporation) issued by the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC). Also, if your plan is to be getting government contracts for your company, a business registration is one of the first requirements.

  8. Perpetual Succession / Continuity

    Another benefits of registration your business with Corporate Affairs Commission is the business continuing even if you die or become ill. A registered business is an entity in its own right; thus, another can assume ownership or control or your business can be sold. Neither is it possible without registration.
